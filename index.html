About the Event

The ARBOR HACKATHON 2025 is designed to cultivate 21st-century skills and promote Arbor's vision of nurturing future-ready learners

What is ARBOR HACKATHON?

The Computer Science Department is proud to present ARBOR HACKATHON 2025, a dynamic two-day innovation challenge aimed at empowering students through creativity, critical thinking, and cutting-edge problem-solving.

With a strong emphasis on real-world application, this event provides a platform for hands-on learning through project-based challenges while encouraging teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving skills.

We propose conducting the event on July 25th (Friday) and July 26th (Saturday), 2025, from 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM, within the school premises.

Participation Guidelines

  • Team Formation: Teams must be formed within the same track (Track 1: Classes 6-9 or Track 2: Classes 10-12)
  • Team Size: Each team must consist of 1 to 3 members, with at least one girl member encouraged
  • Ideas: Up to 3 ideas can be submitted by each team
  • Equipment: Students must bring their own laptops and internet dongles for the event
  • Mobile Phones: Allowed but their usage and safety is solely the responsibility of the students
  • Hardware Projects: Welcomed; however, teams must arrange their own materials/components. The school will not provide hardware supplies
  • Support: On-site mentors will guide and support teams throughout the event

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about ARBOR HACKATHON 2025

Who can participate in ARBOR HACKATHON 2025?

The hackathon is open to all students of Arbor International School from classes 6 to 12. Participants will be divided into two tracks:

  • Track 1: Classes 6-9 (Junior)
  • Track 2: Classes 10-12 (Senior)
What is the team size requirement?

Teams can consist of 1 to 3 members. We encourage teams to include at least one girl member to promote diversity and inclusion.

What should we bring to the hackathon?

Participants should bring:

  • Their own laptops (with necessary software installed)
  • Internet dongles (optional but recommended)
  • Any specific hardware components if working on a hardware project
  • School ID card
  • Notebook and pens for brainstorming
Can we submit multiple ideas?

Yes, each team can submit up to 3 ideas. However, you'll need to focus on developing one primary idea during the hackathon.

What if we don't have coding experience?

No problem! While coding skills are helpful, they're not mandatory. The hackathon welcomes all types of innovative solutions, including:

  • Conceptual designs and prototypes
  • Business models and strategies
  • Physical prototypes (if you bring your own materials)
  • Low-code/no-code solutions

Mentors will be available to guide all participants.

Will food be provided during the event?

Yes, refreshments and lunch will be provided on both days of the hackathon. Please inform us in advance if you have any dietary restrictions.

What are the judging criteria?

Projects will be evaluated based on:

  • Innovation & Creativity (30%): Originality and uniqueness of the solution
  • Relevance to Theme (20%): How well the solution addresses the chosen theme
  • Feasibility & Practicality (20%): Potential for real-world implementation
  • Presentation & Communication (15%): Clarity and effectiveness of the pitch
  • Teamwork & Collaboration (15%): How well the team worked together
